The Competition



PHASE I: Design It

Teams design the model for an autonomous and/or remote-controlled mining robot. Each revisiting team must create a new design every year.



PHASE II: Build It

Teams assemble robots using their mechanical design. The robot must be capable of mining efficiently while moving about a simulated lunar arena before moving to Phase III.



PHASE III: Dig It

After completing all Phase II requirements, select teams will be invited to the Phase III on-site challenge at the Kennedy Space Center (Florida).


About the Competition

Lunabotics, also known as the NASA Robotic Mining Competition, is a year-long competition where university-level teams compete to design, build, and operate robots that conduct operations on a simulated lunar surface. Teams experience the full project life cycle while employing systems engineering principles to meet competition deadlines throughout the year.

Lunabotics robots must navigate a terrain zone, complete with craters and boulders, meant to simulate the extreme conditions that autonomous vehicles will experience during real operations. They then reach the mining zone, where the robots mine deep below the surface to collect icy gravel, prized for its water content. After conducting one mining cycle and collecting as much gravel as they can, the robots must traverse the terrain zone again and deposit their gravel in a collection bin, where it is sifted and weighed.

During the mining competition, teams receive points for the amount of gravel they mine, as well as for autonomous operations and dust-tolerant designs. Teams lose points for mass, bandwidth use, and energy consumed. Throughout the year, teams are also scored on project deliverables such as a project management plan and a public outreach report. The team that scores the highest across all divisions is deemed the winner.

Lunabotics is an interdisciplinary competition that involves students from all disciplines and majors, including engineering, material science, geology, education, business, and more. It also seeks to bring together students from all walks of life by encouraging collaboration and outreach projects with underrepresented communities.
