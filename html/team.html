The Team

Lunabotics is a student-deigned organization of students from all sorts of backgrounds. Due to the range of the competition, we recruit students from various majors, and encourage those interested to join.

The organization is divided into five major teams.

Each team is led by our team executives.


Design

Before anything can be built, our design team is tasked with planning and developing every subsystem and element of the robot.

Adry Lain (Design Lead)


Fabrication

Our fabrication team focuses on the mechanical assembly and testing of all pre-designed elements.

Mike Fox (Fabrication Lead)


Electrical

Our electrical team addresses the power requirements and electromechanical design needs of the robot.

Joey Motto (Electrical Lead)


Programming

The programming sub-team develops programs to allow users to control the robot manually and also give it autonomy. Programming also delves in web design and development to maintain TennesseeLunabotics.com.

Matthew Bowlby (Programming Lead)


Business

Tennessee Lunabotics relies on its business team for financial and outreach matters. Acting as our financial backbone, our business team manages all funding, donations, and sponsorships.

Erin Dunn (Business Lead)


Safety Officer

Our safety officer ensures team members are adhering to Occupational Health and Safety (OHS) Guidelines and industry standards while working on the robot and during team meetings. The safety officer also assists with responsible and safe design choices, conducts risk assessments, and enforces preventive measures to ensure the safety of the team.

Srikar Rairao (Safety Lead)
